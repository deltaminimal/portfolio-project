### Strategy Overview

Cold email deliverability has become highly complex, requiring strict constraints on volume, technical setup, and copy variation. Success is no longer about maximizing raw output, but rather ensuring your messages reliably bypass Email Service Provider (ESP) filters and land directly in the inbox.

### The 10 Deliverability Tips

1. **Cap Individual Sending Volume**
   - Keep volume under 25 emails per day per inbox. Exceeding 30 emails instantly triggers ESP spam filters and causes rapid reputation decay.

2. **Vary Your Email Copy**
   - Avoid generic, repetitive messaging which puts providers like Google on high alert. Use spintax, deep personalization, and continuous A/B testing to keep content unique.

3. **Isolate Domain Reputation**
   - Never use your primary company domain. If a single inbox gets flagged, the entire root domain suffers.

4. **Deploy Secondary Domains**
   - Protect your brand by purchasing alternative domains using variations like "get", "try", or "use" prefixed to your core name.

5. **Authenticate DNS Records**
   - Build baseline trust with ESPs by properly configuring your SPF, DKIM, and DMARC protocols to validate your sending identity.

6. **Scale Safely via Inbox Rotation**
   - Instead of blasting from one account, distribute your volume across 10 to 15 separate inboxes to scale reach while staying under safety thresholds.

7. **Warm Up Inboxes Thoroughly**
   - Increase your inbox placement by mimicking natural human activity through automated warmup tools before launching live campaigns.

8. **Default to Plain Text**
   - Strip all tracking links, images, and heavy formatting from your initial outreach. A plain, human-centric email significantly lowers spam risk.

9. **Target High Delivery Rates**
   - Aim for a minimum 95% inbox placement rate. Use specialized deliverability infrastructure infrastructure like Maildoso to maintain high baselines.

10. **Enforce Strict Data Quality**
    - Keep your bounce rate strictly under 5% to avoid being classified as a spammer. Source data from reputable providers and clean every list with validation tools like Bouncer.
