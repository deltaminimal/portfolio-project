### Strategy Overview

To significantly increase self-sourced qualified pipeline from enterprise Account Executives (AEs), you must move away from marketing-speak. Rebuild your sales talk tracks and outbound emails using the exact language, terminology, and phrasing pulled from real customer conversations.

### The 3-Step Execution Framework

- **Step 1:** Pull raw customer language directly from your historical sales call transcripts.
- **Step 2:** Use AI to analyze the transcripts and structure the insights into a unified outbound framework.
- **Step 3:** Rebuild all sales scripts, talk tracks, and email sequences using this structured customer voice.

---

### The AI Prompt Blueprint: Building the Outbound Squad Messaging Matrix

Use the structured text below to build an automated AI skill or system agent:

```text
Take the attached [resources: sales call transcripts, persona, marketing materials, case studies, etc.] and turn this into an Outbound Squad Messaging Matrix. The messaging should be written using the customer's voice. This messaging matrix should be formatted into a table with these four columns:

1) Priorities
Format this into a statement like this: [headline]. [outcome] + [avoid problem].
- Headline: What is top of mind for your prospect's peers? Imagine a dozen prospects in the same role gathered in a room. What trends are they worried about or focused on? There should be no more than two or three priorities.
- Outcome: What are the specific metrics, outcomes, or KPIs they want to improve?
- Avoid problem: What core operational problem are they hoping to completely avoid or solve?

2) Current solutions
Map out their status quo (how the prospect is currently getting the job done). Note that "solutions" does not just mean technology. Break it down by:
- People: Are they hiring specialized roles, reducing headcount, etc.?
- Process: Are they implementing a specific manual workflow or process?
- Technology: Are they using home-grown tools or a specific competitor?

3) Problems
Identify what gets in the way of their priorities. Ensure you distinguish between simple friction and true business problems:
- Pain (Friction): Minor inconveniences and daily frustrations.
- Problems (Business Impact): The actual, measurable impact of that pain on the broader business. Define this in the customer's exact voice.

4) Aspirations
Define the prospect's desired future state. These must align tightly with the actual macro outcomes your solution provides to existing customers.

This is for: [insert company name] who sells [insert solution] to [insert persona]. Our website is: [insert website URL]. Example clients of theirs are [insert examples].

Once completed, convert the messaging matrix into a Word Document.
```
