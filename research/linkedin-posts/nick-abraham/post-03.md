### Strategy Overview

When managing campaigns at scale, rely on a single primary metric—the **email-to-lead ratio**—to determine whether a campaign simply needs more fuel or requires a complete overhaul.

### The 3-Path Decision Framework

1. **Performing At or Above Average**
   - _Diagnosis:_ The offer and targeting are working.
   - _Action:_ Top off the campaign with net-new leads using previous targeting criteria and lookalike lists. Ensure all leads are enriched and validated. Automate this pipeline to eliminate manual list pulling.

2. **Underperforming by 40%+**
   - _Diagnosis:_ The campaign is broken. Adding more leads will only burn your contact data. This is usually an audience-fit issue rather than a copy issue.
   - _Action:_ Stop the campaign. Take the exact same offer into a completely new vertical with highly relevant messaging, and launch it as a fresh campaign.

3. **Underperforming (Locked Vertical & Rigid Offer)**
   - _Diagnosis:_ You cannot change the offer or pivot to a new industry.
   - _Action:_ Your only option is to iterate on email copy and test alternative angles. This path has the lowest probability of success, and clients should be notified of the low upside upfront.

---

> ### 📊 Scale Advice
>
> Compare every individual campaign against your overall agency benchmark. Use the email-to-lead ratio as your primary signal to dictate logic and prevent wasted ad or data spend.
