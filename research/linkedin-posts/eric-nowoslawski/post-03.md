### Strategy Overview

When a campaign hits a 0.3% reply rate and zero positive conversions after two weeks, the solution is not to add complexity. You must systematically strip away variables to isolate the point of failure, tighten your market focus, and accelerate copy experimentation.

### The 4-Step Campaign Triage Framework

#### 1. Isolate the Deliverability Variable

Mixed domain lists hide technical filtering issues. If Microsoft is routing you to spam while Google delivers to the primary inbox, aggregated stats mask the breakdown.

- **The Tactic:** Filter your target list to include _only_ Google Workspace accounts (exclude Gmail, Outlook, Proofpoint, and other enterprise filters).
- **Technical Audit:** Run dedicated inbox placement tests, verify blacklists, and audit core domain records: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.

#### 2. Hyper-Segment Down to the Core

Broad lists dilute your value proposition. Shrink your audience down to an incredibly tight cohort where the list criteria naturally dictates the copy.

- **The Tactic:** Whittle your active lead list down to a maximum of 1,000 highly targeted prospects. Apply strict structural filters to match the exact profile of your highest-value active clients.

#### 3. Accelerate Low-Horizon Testing Velocity

Stop trying to build permanent, long-term sequences when a campaign is actively failing. Focus entirely on short-term data collection.

- **The Tactic:** Run multiple parallel micro-campaigns to rapidly test different strategic angles.
- **A/B Variables:** Contrast pain-based vs. value-based angles, target different internal personas, and swap out calls to action.
- **Upfront Value:** Use low-friction offers—like automated lead magnets or tailored competitive intelligence reports—to deliver value before asking for a calendar invite.

#### 4. Pivot from Meeting Asks to Hand-Raisers

Directly asking for a meeting requires high buyer intent and trust. If your core booking CTA is failing, lower the barrier to entry to capture latent interest.

- **The Tactic:** Switch from a direct meeting request to a low-friction "hand-raiser" CTA. Offer high-value resources that buyers will say yes to, even if they are not yet ready to commit to a live conversation.

---

> ### 💡 Key Takeaway
>
> A flatlining outbound campaign is a diagnostic signal. Strip away the noise, isolate your technical delivery to a single inbox provider, narrow your audience list to a hyper-focused segment, and lower conversion friction by trading immediate value for simple hand-raiser responses.
