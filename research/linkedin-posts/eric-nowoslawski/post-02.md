### Strategy Overview

A subject line has exactly one job: earn the open. High-performing outbound campaigns leverage two primary psychology frameworks to bypass immediate deletion filters.

### Framework 1: Internal Collegial Familiarity

This approach mimics the informal, casual nature of internal company communications or customer notes. By stripping away corporate stiffness, the text slips past the brain's defensive "cold email" filter.

- **"pizza order"** (Used for a restaurant platform; replicates urgent internal operational notes).
- **"sales reports"** (Looks like an internal data delivery from a teammate).
- **"quick question"** (A highly debated classic that consistently retains baseline open utility).

### Framework 2: The Curiosity-Driven Offer Gap

This framework pairs an abrupt, specific subject title with a high-value preview text snippet to create an information gap that forces an open.

- **Subject Line:** `"[Company]'s competitors"`
- **Preview Text:** `"I have a list of people engaging with {{competitor}}..."`

### What to Avoid

Never pitch features or pricing in the title. Subject lines like _"50% Off Our Platform"_ signal automated marketing automation instantly, causing buyers to delete the thread without reviewing the body copy.

---

> ### 💡 Key Takeaway
>
> Great subject lines never try to close a sale. They either replicate the relaxed, low-friction format of a message from someone the prospect already knows, or they explicitly hint at critical, time-sensitive industry insights that the buyer cannot afford to miss.
