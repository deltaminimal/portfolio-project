### Strategy Overview

It is completely disingenuous to treat basic digital activity as a definitive "intent to buy." Relying solely on these surface-level signals creates false positives, burns resources, and forces you to compete for a tiny slice of the market. Instead, build a predictable engine by prioritizing static ideal customer profile (ICP) fit first, testing for maximum resonance, and layer in dynamic signals only as a secondary prioritization tool.

### The Breakdown of Signal Myths vs. Reality

- **The Reality of "Signals"**
  - Minor actions (like visiting a client account, following a page, or commenting on a post) are rarely buyer intent. In fact, up to 90% of engagement often comes from competitors or supportive friends within the industry.
  - Internal company changes, such as hiring a junior employee, do not automatically mean leadership is ready to overhaul their entire system architecture.
  - While signal-based outreach feels relevant and can pressure prospects into polite conversations, it rarely correlates to a true pipeline.

- **The TAM Constraint**
  - At any given time, only 3% of your market is actively buying, and 7% is open to it.
  - Treating signals as your primary strategy confines you to a total addressable market (TAM) slice that is simply too small to sustain long-term growth targets. It is often used as a short-term crutch when teams lack bigger strategic ideas.

---

### 🛠️ Execution Framework

1. **Prioritize Static Fit over Dynamic Fit**
   Anchor your targeting on firmographic and technographic data that defines a high-value account before looking at behavioral data.

2. **Run the Core Go-To-Market Sequence**
   Organize campaigns strictly by **Market → Segment → Persona → Angle**. Run distinct tests across these layers to find and maximize message resonance first.

3. **Reconcile Prioritization Logic**
   Once resonance is established, integrate dynamic signals strictly to score accounts. Establish clear rules on whether a mid-fit account with high signals ranks above or below a high-fit account with low signals.

---

> ### 📊 Scale Advice
>
> Aim at goals that help you win long-term, not tactics that absolve you from solving the difficult problem of outbound origination. No company builds sustainable scale solely on behavioral signals; you must actively originate your customer acquisition.
