### Strategy Overview

High cold email volume has trained enterprise buyers to instinctively spot and delete promotional messages. To bypass this initial filter, drop long, pitch-heavy headlines and replace them with short, one- or two-word subject lines that naturally describe the topic without triggering marketing defense mechanisms.

### The Contrast: Pitch-Heavy vs. Topic-Focused

| Avoid: Pitch-Heavy Subject Lines (Triggers Deletion)          | Use: Short, Topic-Focused Subject Lines (Drives Opens) |
| :------------------------------------------------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------- |
| "Automating financial reports"                                | **"Reports"**                                          |
| "Missing attribution data?"                                   | **"Attribution"**                                      |
| "Ramp your reps faster"                                       | **"Ramp"**                                             |
| "Quick question about your freight broker"                    | **"Broker"**                                           |
| "Reduce breach risks without adding more tech"                | **"Breach risk"**                                      |
| "When training completion doesn’t result in behaviour change" | **"Completion rates"**                                 |

### Why Minimalist Subject Lines Perform Better

- **Accurate Labeling Without Deception:** They act as neutral, professional subject matter indicators rather than clickbait. They prepare the reader for the topic without resorting to trickery.
- **Eliminating Sales Telemetry:** Long subject lines that include questions or value propositions act as immediate indicators of automated outbound software. Shorter phrases look like internal communications.
- **Preventing Instinctive Deletion:** Modern professionals process crowded inboxes at high speeds. Stripping away marketing-heavy words removes the obvious cues that tell prospects to delete an email before reading it.

---

> ### 💡 Key Takeaway
>
> Your subject line has exactly one job: to get the email opened. Stop trying to pitch your product or explain your value proposition in the title. Keep the subject line limited to a single topic word or phrase, and leave the heavy lifting to your actual body copy.
