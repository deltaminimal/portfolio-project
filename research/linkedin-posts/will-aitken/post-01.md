### Strategy Overview

Most sales development reps fail with intent data because they track the same surface-level triggers as their competitors (e.g., job changes, funding rounds, general hiring). This causes extreme buyer fatigue. To stand out, you must increase target specificity, look for hidden data points, and layer proximity data to build a genuine point of view.

### Four Advanced Rules for Intent Signals

#### 1. Prioritize Deep Contextual Specificity

Surface observations carry no weight. Injecting exact context into your opening lines creates instant professional credibility.

- **Weak / Automated Phrasing:** "Saw you're hiring."
- **Strong / Specific Phrasing:** "Looks like you're hiring 4 SecOps roles with a SOC2 focus."

#### 2. Target Low-Noise "White Space" Signals

The easier an outbound trigger is to find on LinkedIn or Crunchbase, the more crowded your prospect's inbox will be. Focus on deeper data sources that competitors overlook:

- Granular details tucked inside active job descriptions.
- Product roadmap updates and release notes.
- Negative G2 or third-party platform reviews of your competitors.
- Executive insights shared during founder podcast interviews.
- Under-the-radar tech stack additions or infrastructure removals.

#### 3. Leverage Proximity as an Unfair Advantage

Isolate "warm-adjacent" accounts where buyers are already familiar with your brand, executives, or customer base to break down cold outreach resistance.

- _Key Proximity Points:_ Target lookalike accounts, past webinar attendees, prospects who have been through a historical deal cycle, or users actively liking your corporate content.
- _Crucial Operational Warning:_ Never substitute proximity for structural relevance. Brand familiarity does not automatically mean a prospect has a budget or a pressing need for your solution.

#### 4. Stack Multiple Signals to Form a Point of View

Do not write a cold email based on a single trigger event. Combine multiple operational data points across a single account. Stacking signals isolates elite opportunities and naturally dictates your sales copy.

- **Single Signal (Weak):** "You’re hiring."
- **Stacked Signals (Strong):** "You’ve grown your ops team 30% in the last 90 days, launched a new product, and are actively hiring compliance roles."

#### Proximity Signals

These are signals that suggest this customer is more familiar with you, your company, your executives, your partners, or your existing customers. They can be used to find the warmest leads and open with some element of recognition that overcomes some of the resistance to cold outreach.

- Previously a customer / Churned customer

- Previously booked a meeting
- Replied to past outreach
- Trialed product / Logged in multiple times
- Viewed pricing page
- Viewed product pages repeatedly
- Clicked links in emails
- Downloaded lead magnet / whitepaper
- Attended webinar
- Opened multiple emails / engaged with sequences
- Registered but didn't attend webinar
- Viewed case studies
- Signed up for product updates
- Engaged with outbound rep before
- Engaged with retargeting ads
- Shared your content internally
- Engaged with you / your company's content (liked, commented, tagged someone)
- Follows you / your company
- Viewed your profile
- Referral / came from a referral
- Met at event / attended same conference
- Connected with teammate or founder
- Mutual connections (you or customers)
- Same alumni / community (Slack, LinkedIn group)
