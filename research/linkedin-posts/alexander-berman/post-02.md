### Strategy Overview

Even for high-ticket B2B companies, cold email shouldn't be the immediate default. Before building out complex outbound infrastructure, business owners must exhaust their immediate network and maximize warm lead channels.

### The Low-Hanging Fruit Checklist

Before launching cold outreach, ensure you have addressed these foundational revenue drivers:

- **Speed-to-Lead:** Are you calling every single inbound lead within 60 seconds of submission?
- **Database Remarketing:** Have you gathered all historical contact form inputs and funneled them into an email list (using tools like AWeber)?
- **Referral Generation:** Have you explicitly asked your current clients to recommend at least one new client today?
- **Pricing Optimization:** When was the last time you raised your prices to reflect your actual value?

### The "Revive the Funnel" Text Script

If you sell high-ticket services, you often only need a handful of new clients to fundamentally shift your revenue. Tap your existing pipeline immediately by sending this text message to every dormant prospect in your contact book:

> "Hey [Name], are we still moving forward with the [project name]?"
> _Example: "Hey Mike, are we still moving forward with the SEO project?"_

### When to Transition to Cold Email Scale

Only shift your focus to cold outbound campaigns once you have completely squeezed your existing warm channels and met the following criteria:

1.  Your core offer is locked in and thoroughly validated.
2.  Your pricing model is optimized for high margins while remaining highly attractive to potential buyers.
3.  Your internal sales systems are fast and tight enough to handle a heavy influx of new prospects.

Once these components are proven, scale your outreach by feeding verified data sources (like the 4.9 million+ B2B contacts in the ScraperCity Lead Database) into your finalized sales funnel.
