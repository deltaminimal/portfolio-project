### Strategy Overview

Prospects instinctively put their guard up when they feel they are being actively sold, persuaded, or corrected. To bypass this resistance, replace direct value statements with strategic questions that lead prospects to discover their own operational gaps.

### Telling vs. Discovery

#### The Pitfall of "Telling"

Directly pitching your capabilities sounds like a typical presentation:

> "We help families protect assets from probate by making sure trusts are properly funded."

When you tell a prospect what you do, it feels like an attempt to handle or steer them. It forces a conclusion onto them, triggering immediate defense mechanisms.

#### The Power of "Discovery"

Framing the same concept as a question completely shifts the dynamic:

> "You’re probably already on top of this, but when your trust was set up, were the assets actually retitled into the trust, or was the pour-over will intended to catch anything left outside of it?"

This approach invites reflection instead of forcing an answer. It prompts a critical internal dialogue within the prospect's mind:

- _"Wait… were all our assets actually moved?"_
- _"How does my trust actually work under the hood?"_
- _"Could our attorney have missed something?"_

---

> ### 💡 Key Takeaway
>
> People rarely believe what they are told, but they always believe what they discover for themselves. Stop trying to corner prospects into agreeing with your pitch. Instead, use "poke-the-bear" questions to help them notice a risk, vulnerability, or inefficiency they hadn't considered before.
